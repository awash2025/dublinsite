May 8, 2024

During my trip to the National Museum of Ireland, I had the incredible opportunity to see fossils of extinct birds, rabbits, bugs, and sea creatures. It was like stepping back in time! The museum's vast collection included an array of fascinating specimens, each telling a unique story about life millions of years ago. The detailed displays and informative plaques provided captivating insights into how these creatures lived and adapted to their environments. Seeing the delicate structures of ancient birds, the intricate details of prehistoric bugs, and the remains of sea creatures was truly awe-inspiring. The museum also had interactive exhibits and engaging videos that made the whole experience both educational and fun. It,s amazing to think about how much history is preserved in these fossils. If you're a fan of natural history or just curious about the ancient past, this museum is definitely worth a visit!

May 13, 2024

I found the National Disablilty Authority of Ireland's work incredibly inspiring. The NDA is dedicated to promoting equal opportunities for people with disabilities and ensuring their full participation in society. They conduct research, provide policy advice, and develop practical tools to enhance accessibility and inclusion. It was enlightening to learn the different ways that the USA promotes accessibility vs Ireland and the on going debate on who does it better. This experience has deepened my understanding of disability rights and the importance of advocacy.

May 18-19, 2024

Over the weekend, we took a mini trip to Galway and the Cliffs of Moher. Our visit to the Computer and Communications Museum of Ireland was so much fun. We explored the timeline of electronics, which was fascinating and nostalgic, seeing how far technology has come. Experiencing true Irish nightlife was a highlight as we enjoyed live performances and traditional music. The next day, we drove two hours to the Cliffs of Moher, where we witnessed breathtaking views of the North Atlantic Ocean and the historic farms affected by the famine. As we rode through the mountains, one of our instructors shared a poignant remark: "These are beautiful but unforgiving views, as they took the lives of one million people and caused starvation and emigration." This trip was both an educational and memorable experience. on he way to the cliffs we stopped to see the Dunguaire Castle.

May 20, 2024

Today we went to the little musum of dublin and even though its little the knowlegde they gave was very big and impactful. We dove into the history of Ireland, and one of the most eye-opening parts was learning about the Great Famine. The exhibits really brought the past to life, showing us how this tragic period shaped the country. The museum's engaging displays and passionate guides made the history feel so real and relevant. It was a fascinating and moving experience that gave me a deeper understanding of Ireland's history and resilience.
